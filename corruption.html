Insights

This visualization shows how countries with stronger institutional quality, measured by CPI, tend to concentrate more billionaire wealth overall, while also revealing notable exceptions. Some nations accumulate large fortunes despite weaker CPI scores, suggesting that factors such as market size, natural resources, or rapid economic growth can offset institutional weaknesses. The pattern highlights that institutions are especially important for sustaining and protecting wealth at scale, rather than determining whether wealth exists at all.

Billionaires, Prosperity, and Institutions

This project analyzes global billionaire concentration and wealth, and how they relate to GDP per capita, institutional quality (CPI), and gender inequality. Each visualization includes three key insights.

Home Economics Corruption Gender

Viz 01 • Billionaire Count by Country

Choropleth map — concentration of billionaires

Insights

  • Billionaires are concentrated in a small set of countries rather than evenly distributed globally.
  • Large financial and innovation ecosystems appear linked to higher billionaire presence.
  • Many countries show near-zero counts, illustrating extreme global inequality at the top end.

Viz 02 • Total Billionaire Wealth by Country

Choropleth map — trillions USD (scale-correct)

Insights

  • Total billionaire wealth is even more concentrated than billionaire counts.
  • A few countries dominate global private wealth, with clear outliers at the top.
  • This complements Viz 01 by measuring “wealth intensity,” not just headcount.

Viz 05 • GDP per Capita, CPI, and Billionaire Count

Bubble chart — prosperity + institutions + billionaire concentration

Insights

  • Countries with higher GDP per capita generally host more billionaires.
  • Higher CPI (lower corruption) often aligns with larger billionaire presence, but exceptions exist.
  • Outliers suggest billionaire ecosystems depend on multiple factors beyond prosperity alone.
